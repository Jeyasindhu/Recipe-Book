Butter Chicken

About Meal

Butter Chicken is a rich and creamy curry featuring tender, marinated chicken pieces cooked in a spiced tomato sauce enriched with butter and cream. The chicken is first marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, then cooked until perfectly charred. It's simmered in a luscious sauce made with tomato puree, cream, and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), giving it a unique and delightful flavor. Garnished with fresh coriander, this dish is a staple in Indian cuisine and beloved worldwide.

Ingredients:


500g boneless chicken, cut into cubes
1 cup yogurt
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons butter
1 cup tomato puree
1/2 cup cream
1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
Salt to taste

Instruction:


1. In a bowl, mix together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt.
2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated.
3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse into the chicken.
4. Heat 1 tablespoon of butter in a pan over medium heat.
5. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are fully cooked and slightly charred, stirring occasionally.
6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pan and set it aside.
7. In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter.
8. Add the tomato puree and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the puree thickens slightly.
9. Add the cream, kasuri methi, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine.
10. Add the cooked chicken pieces back into the pan with the sauce.
11. Simmer the mixture for 5-10 minutes over low heat, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken slightly.
12. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
Once the Butter Chicken is ready, garnish it with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
13. Serve hot with naan bread, rice, or your preferred side dish.
14. Enjoy your homemade Butter Chicken!

Palak Paneer

About Meal

Palak Paneer is a popular North Indian vegetarian dish featuring paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a smooth, vibrant green spinach (palak) sauce. This nutritious and flavorful dish combines the mild taste of paneer with the rich, slightly tangy flavor of pureed spinach, seasoned with aromatic spices. It's a favorite in Indian households and restaurants, known for its striking color and creamy texture.

Ingredients:


500g fresh spinach leaves (palak)
2 green chilies
250g paneer, cubed
1 tablespoon oil or ghee
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1/2 cup cream or yogurt
Salt to taste
Fresh cream (optional)
Fresh coriander leaves

Instruction:


1.Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
2. Add the spinach leaves and cook for 2-3 minutes until wilted.
3. Drain the spinach and immediately transfer it to ice-cold water to retain its green color.
4.Drain again and blend the spinach along with green chilies into a smooth puree. Set aside.
Heat 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
6. Add the paneer cubes and fry them until golden brown on all sides. Remove and set aside.
7. In the same pan, add 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee and heat it over medium heat.
8. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
9. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
10. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
11. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
12.Add the spinach puree to the masala and mix well.
13.Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
14. Add garam masala and the fried paneer cubes.
15.Mix gently to coat the paneer with the spinach sauce.
16. Simmer for another 5-7 minutes.
17. Stir in the cream or yogurt and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
18. Garnish with a drizzle of fresh cream and chopped coriander leaves if desired.
19. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.
20. Enjoy your Palak Paneer, a delightful and healthy vegetarian dish that’s sure to please everyone!

Chickpea Curry

About Meal

Chole, also known as Chickpea Curry, is a beloved dish from North India. It features chickpeas (chole) simmered in a rich, flavorful gravy made with onions, tomatoes, and an array of aromatic spices. This hearty and nutritious curry is traditionally served with rice, bhature (fried bread), or roti. The combination of tender chickpeas and a spicy, tangy sauce makes it a favorite in Indian cuisine, offering a delightful blend of textures and flavors.

Ingredients:


2 cups dried chickpeas (or 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed)
Water for soaking and cooking
Salt to taste
2 tablespoon oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
2 green chilies, slit
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Instruction:


1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them in water overnight. Drain and rinse them.
2. Cook the soaked chickpeas in a pressure cooker with enough water and a pinch of salt until they are soft and tender. If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them, then proceed to the next step.
3. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat.
4. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
5. Add chopped onions and cook until they turn golden brown.
6. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
7. Add pureed tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder.
8. Cook the mixture until the oil separates from the masala.
9. Add the cooked chickpeas to the masala and mix well.
10. Add garam masala and amchur. Stir to combine.
11. Add salt to taste and simmer the curry for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
12. If the curry is too thick, you can add a little water to reach the desired consistency.
13. Garnish the Chole with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
14. Serve hot with rice, bhature, roti, or naan.
15. Enjoy your Chole, a comforting and flavorful dish that's perfect for a satisfying meal!

Aloo-Gobi Masala

About Meal

Aloo-Gobi Masala is a classic vegetarian dish from North India that features potatoes (aloo) and cauliflower (gobi) cooked together in a fragrant blend of spices. This dry curry is a staple in Indian households, offering a delicious combination of tender potatoes and cauliflower florets coated in a spicy, flavorful masala. It's a versatile dish that can be served with roti, paratha, or rice, making it a perfect option for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:


2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
1 medium cauliflower, cut into florets
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, finely chopped
2 green chilies, slit
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon cumin powder
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instruction:


1. Heat oil in a large pan or skillet over medium heat.
2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
3. Add the finely chopped onions to the pan and sauté until they turn golden brown.
4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the spices are well incorporated.
7. Add the cubed potatoes to the pan and mix well to coat them with the spice mixture.
8. Cover the pan and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure even cooking.
9. Add the cauliflower florets and mix well. Cover and cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes and cauliflower are tender.
10. Sprinkle garam masala over the cooked vegetables and mix well.
11. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
12. Garnish the Aloo-Gobi Masala with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
13. Serve hot with roti, paratha, or rice.
14.Enjoy your Aloo-Gobi Masala, a hearty and flavorful vegetarian dish that’s sure to be a hit at any meal!

Chicken Biryani

About Meal

Chicken Biryani is a popular and aromatic Indian dish that combines succulent pieces of chicken with basmati rice, spices, and herbs. This one-pot meal is known for its rich flavors and the beautiful balance between the tender chicken and fragrant rice. Traditionally cooked in a layered fashion, biryani is a celebration of spices and is often served on special occasions or festive gatherings.

Ingredients:


2 cups basmati rice 500g chicken
cut into pieces 2 large onions
thinly sliced 2 tomatoes
chopped 1/2 cup yogurt
2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste 2 green chilies slit
1/4 cup fresh coriander leaveschopped
1/4 cup fresh mint leaves chopped
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
2 teaspoons red chili powder
1 tablespoon garam masala
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
4 cloves
4 green cardamom pods
2 bay leaves
1-inch cinnamon stick
4 tablespoons oil or ghee
1 cup fried onions (for garnish)
Salt to taste
Saffron threads soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk (optional)

Instruction:


1.Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes.
2. Boil water in a large pot, add salt, and cook the soaked rice until it is 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
3. In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
4. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat.
5. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, bay leaves, and the cinnamon stick. Saute until fragrant.
6. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
7. Add the marinated chicken, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Cook until the chicken is browned and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
8. Add garam masala and mix well.
9. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, spread a layer of the partially cooked rice at the bottom.
10. Add a layer of the cooked chicken with its gravy.
11. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of rice on top.
12.If using, drizzle the saffron milk over the top layer of rice for added flavor and color.
13. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to seal in the steam.
14.Place the sealed pot over low heat and cook for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
15. Remove from heat and let it rest, covered, for another 10 minutes.
16. Fluff the rice gently with a fork.
17. Garnish with fried onions and fresh coriander leaves.
18. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a simple salad.
19. Enjoy your Chicken Biryani, a delicious and aromatic dish that's perfect for special occasions or a delightful family meal!

Red Lentil Curry

About Meal

PMasoor Dal, also known as Red Lentil Curry, is a comforting and nutritious Indian dish made with red lentils simmered in a flavorful mixture of onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. This hearty curry is known for its simplicity and quick preparation, making it a staple in many Indian households. The creamy texture of the lentils combined with the warming spices creates a delicious and satisfying meal, often served with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.

Ingredients:


1 cup red lentils (masoor dal)
1 tablespoon oil or ghee
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, chopped
2 green chilies, slit
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
4 cups water
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves

Instruction:


1. Rinse the red lentils under cold water until the water runs clear.
2. In a large pot, add the lentils and 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the lentils are soft and cooked through, about 20-25 minutes. Skim off any foam that forms on the surface. Set aside.
3. In a large pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat.
4. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
5. Add the chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
6. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
7. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
8. Add the cooked lentils along with their cooking water to the pan with the masala. Mix well.
9. Add salt to taste and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
10. Stir in garam masala and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
11. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
12. Garnish the Masoor Dal with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
13. Serve hot with steamed rice, roti, or naan.
14. Enjoy your Masoor Dal, a delicious and nutritious red lentil curry that’s perfect for a wholesome meal!

Kidney Bean Curry

About Meal

Rajma, or Kidney Bean Curry, is a popular North Indian dish made with red kidney beans simmered in a thick, flavorful tomato-based gravy. This hearty and nutritious curry is known for its rich and comforting taste, making it a favorite comfort food in Indian households. Often served with rice (rajma chawal) or roti, Rajma is a wholesome and satisfying meal that combines the protein-rich beans with aromatic spices and herbs.

Ingredients:


2 cups dried red kidney beans (or 2 cans of kidney beans, drained and rinsed)
Water for soaking and cooking
2 tablespoons oil or ghee
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
2 large onions, finely chopped
2 tomatoes, pureed
2 green chilies, slit
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)t
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves

Instruction:


1. If using dried kidney beans, rinse them and soak them in water overnight. Drain and rinse again.
2. Cook the soaked beans in a pressure cooker with enough water and a pinch of salt until they are soft and tender. If using canned kidney beans, drain and rinse them, then proceed to the next step.
3. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat.
4. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
5. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
6. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and cook for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
7. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook the mixture until the oil separates from the masala.
8. Add the cooked kidney beans to the masala and mix well.
9. Add garam masala and amchur. Stir to combine.
10.Add salt to taste and simmer the curry for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If the curry is too thick, you can add a little water to reach the desired consistency.
11. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
12. Garnish the Rajma with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
13. Serve hot with steamed rice (rajma chawal), roti, or naan.
14. Enjoy your Rajma, a comforting and flavorful kidney bean curry that's perfect for a hearty meal!

Tandoori Chicken

About Meal

Tandoori Chicken is a popular Indian dish consisting of chicken marinated in a mixture of yogurt and aromatic spices, then roasted to perfection. Traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven), the chicken achieves a smoky flavor and a charred exterior while remaining tender and juicy inside. The marinade, typically red due to the use of chili powder and sometimes food coloring, infuses the chicken with a rich, complex flavor. Tandoori Chicken is often served with naan, rice, and a side of fresh salad, making it a complete and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:


1 cup yogurt
2 tablespoons lemon juice
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional, for color and smokiness)
Salt to taste
Red food coloring (optional, for traditional red color)
4-6 chicken legs or thighs, skinless
Lemon wedges
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Sliced onions

Instruction:


1. Make deep slits in the chicken pieces to allow the marinade to penetrate.
2. In a large bowl, mix together the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, smoked paprika (if using), salt, and red food coloring (if using).
3. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight for best results.
4. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). If you have a grill function, you can use it to achieve a more authentic char.
5. Line a baking tray with aluminum foil and place a wire rack on top. Arrange the marinated chicken pieces on the rack.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes, turning the pieces halfway through, until the chicken is cooked through and has a slight char. For a more authentic flavor, you can grill the chicken on an outdoor grill or use a broiler function in your oven.
7. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes.
8. Garnish with lemon wedges, chopped coriander leaves, and sliced onions.
9. Serve the Tandoori Chicken hot with naan, rice, or as part of a larger Indian meal.
10. Enjoy your Tandoori Chicken, a flavorful and aromatic dish that’s sure to be a hit with family and friends!

Samosa

About Meal

Samosa is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crisp, flaky pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. This triangular-shaped delicacy is deep-fried to golden perfection, making it irresistibly crunchy on the outside and deliciously flavorful on the inside. Samosas are often served with chutneys such as mint or tamarind and are a favorite at parties, street stalls, and as an appetizer in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients:


2 cups all-purpose flour
1/4 cup oil or ghee
1/2 teaspoon salt
Water (as needed)
3 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
1 tablespoon oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon mustard seeds (optional)
1 onion, finely chopped
2 green chilies, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon coriander powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or 1 tablespoon lemon juice
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Oil for deep frying

Instruction:


1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour and salt.
2. Add the oil or ghee and rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
3. Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead to form a firm, smooth dough.
4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
5. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
6. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds (if using) and let them splutter.
7. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
8. Add the green chilies and ginger-garlic paste, and cook for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
9. Add the peas and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
10. Add the mashed potatoes, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur (or lemon juice), and salt. Mix well to combine.
11. Cook for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is well heated and the spices are blended.
12. Stir in the chopped coriander leaves and remove from heat. Let the filling cool.
13. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
14. Roll out each ball into an oval or circle, about 6 inches in diameter.
15. Cut the rolled dough in half to form two semi-circles.
16. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with a little water.
17. Fill the cone with 1-2 tablespoons of the potato filling.
18. Seal the top edge of the cone by pinching the edges together, ensuring there are no gaps. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
19. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
20. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the samosas in batches and fry until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides.
21. Remove the fried samosas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
22. Serve the hot samosas with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or your favorite dipping sauce.
23. Enjoy your Samosas, a delightful and savory snack that’s perfect for any occasion!

Dosa

About Meal

Dosa is a traditional South Indian dish that resembles a thin, crispy pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram lentils). It is renowned for its delicate texture and slightly tangy flavor due to the fermentation process. Dosas are typically served hot with a variety of accompaniments such as sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney. They are a staple breakfast item in South India but are enjoyed at any time of the day across India and around the world.

Ingredients:


2 cups rice (preferably parboiled rice)
1 cup urad dal (split black gram lentils)
1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
Water (for soaking and grinding)
Salt to taste
Oil or ghee (for cooking the dosas)

Instruction:


1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under cold water until the water runs clear.
2. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds in one bowl and the urad dal in another bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours, or overnight.
3. Drain the soaked urad dal and grind it to a smooth and fluffy batter, adding a little water as needed.
4. Drain the soaked rice and fenugreek seeds and grind them to a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
5. Combine both batters in a large bowl, mix well, and add salt to taste.
6. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight until it doubles in volume and becomes slightly frothy.
7. Stir the fermented batter gently. If it is too thick, add a little water to achieve a pourable consistency.
8. Heat a non-stick skillet or a traditional cast-iron dosa tawa (griddle) over medium-high heat.
9. Once hot, drizzle a few drops of oil and spread it with a cloth or paper towel to lightly grease the surface.
10. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the tawa and quickly spread it out in a circular motion with the back of the ladle to form a thin, even layer.
11. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges and on top of the dosa.
12. Cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy. Flip if you prefer it cooked on both sides, although traditional dosas are usually cooked on one side only.
13. Serve the hot dosa immediately with sambar, coconut chutney, or any other preferred accompaniments.
14. Enjoy your Dosa, a delicious and crispy South Indian delight that's perfect for any meal!
